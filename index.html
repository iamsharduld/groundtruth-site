The problem

It’s not missing reports. It’s missing proof.

Every project files a report. But the company being graded pays for its own report card, and the numbers come from the very people who did the work. Almost no one independently checks what really happened on the ground.

  • ₹34,909crore spent on social causes in a single year — by law. India, FY2023–24 · MCA
  • 18% of large companies explain how they actually measured their impact. KPMG India, 2024
  • 7.5% of trees survived in one flagship government planting drive. Government audit (CAG)

And new rules now require companies to prove their results independently — so honest, low-cost verification is about to matter far more than it does today.

Why now

The rules just changed — proof is now the law.

For years a company could simply report its own results and move on. A wave of new rules now says someone independent has to check them — which is exactly the gap this fills.

  1. 2021

    An outside check became mandatory

    Large social-spending projects must now be assessed by someone independent of the company that ran them.

    Companies Act · CSR Rule 8(3)
  2. 2024

    Paid for trees that survive

    Tree-planting credits are now earned for canopy that actually lives — not for the number of saplings put in the ground.

    Green Credit Programme
  3. by 2027

    The biggest companies must be assured

    India’s 1,000 largest listed firms must have their environmental and social numbers independently checked.

    SEBI · BRSR Core

How it works

No site visit. No special hardware. Three steps.

  1. 1

    Point it at the place

  2. 2

  3. 3

The full picture

One check can be fooled. Three, lined up, can’t — as easily.

A satellite is powerful, but it only sees greenness. So we cross-check what we see from space against what people on the ground say, and against what the project itself claimed — and only call it verified when the three agree.

  • From space

    Did the land green up?

    Free satellite photos, every few days, going back years — on the exact plot, all year round.

  • From people

    Did it really happen?

    Short, automated phone calls ask the people it was meant to help — gathered independently of the company.

  • From records

    What was claimed?

    The project’s own report is the thing under test — lined up against what the land and the people say.

When the three don’t line up, that gap is exactly what this is built to surface.

Common questions

Fair questions, plainly answered.

Is the satellite data really free?

Yes. The images come from the European Space Agency’s Sentinel-2 satellites and are public. Anyone can look them up — no account, no fee — which is why the check can run all year instead of once.

Can’t a project just fake the green?

It’s hard to fake well. We look at the exact plot, across the whole year, and check that the green is real, settled canopy — not a quick cover crop or a borrowed patch of the forest next door.

What about monsoon clouds?

Clouds do hide the ground in the rainy months. We sample across the seasons and, where it matters, add radar that sees straight through cloud — so a real green-up isn’t missed.

Which kinds of projects can you check?

Anything whose result shows on the land — planted forests, restored ground, water bodies. Schools and health programmes are checked through the phone-call and records steps instead.

Does this replace a field visit?

No — it makes field visits count. It runs all year for almost nothing and points to the projects worth visiting, instead of relying on a single yearly snapshot.
